ABSTRACT

This paper presents the technical architecture of the TCS Green Safe House autonomous energy platform and the KISWARM distributed AI infrastructure. The system demonstrates practical implementation of swarm intelligence for energy grid management, achieving 100% energy autonomy through distributed AI agents operating across a mesh network. We document the convergence of photovoltaic generation, intelligent storage management, and autonomous demand response in an open, verifiable system architecture.

1. INTRODUCTION

Modern energy infrastructure faces fundamental challenges: centralization creates single points of failure, grid instability increases with renewable penetration, and existing demand-response systems lack the autonomy necessary for true energy independence.

We address these challenges through a dual approach: a physical energy platform (TCS Green Safe House) providing autonomous generation and storage, and a distributed AI system (KISWARM) coordinating the swarm intelligence layer.
